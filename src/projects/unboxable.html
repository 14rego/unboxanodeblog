Cutting Edge

I can tell you're as excited to hear about all the underlying technology as I am to tell you about it. But there's nothing exceptional about this web site, I'll admit. It's HTML, CSS, and a tiny bit* of javascript. And that's the beauty of it to me. What else does it need? Not a thing.

While the final product is just a few HTML files, the build process is a bit more robust than you might expect. While developing locally, I rely on Node to serve my files. I also employ Gulp to do things like compile my CSS from SCSS files, lint, concatenate, and minify my JS, and piece together HTML files I build in a very MVC-sort-of style with global page templates and view partials.

Once the build and compilation is complete, I just serve my HTML files statically on a linux server with Apache mod-rewrite rules for pretty URLs.

The blog does, however, rely on an API I created. No way I'm drawing up all 50 of the blog posts in my archive manually. Check out the Unboxapi project here.

Of course, I have plans to rewrite this site in React immediately after "finishing" it.

*Let's be honest, even a "tiny bit" is probably overkill in terms of user experience. Do things really need to zoom around and have cool effects? Not really.

Ain't It Pretty

Again, there's nothing particularly exceptional here. I really needed to spend more time developing than designing this site because a redesign was well overdue. Every project has it's priorities and the design for this simply wasn't it. So, I found an open-source design template called "Black" by UICookies and modified it until it was exactly what I needed.
