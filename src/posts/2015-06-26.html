Following are my take-aways from the 2015 Kansas City Developers Conference

Build and API in Node in under 30 mins
Jordan Kasper

I was hoping for more of an emphasis on “You can do it in 30 minutes because you just don’t know how simple it really is.” Instead of… “I work at this company that makes this tool that turns this complicated thing into something that you can do in a short amount of time - and, of course - I’m going to do it as fast as possible to be sure it’s in under 30 minutes.”

It was great to see a tool being used by someone who knows the “ins and outs“ of the tool, if for no other reason. Cool tool, sure. I’m not sold that easily.

Overcoming Chartophobia
Meagan Longoria

While I was hoping for lots of examples showing great alternatives for complicated tables full of data, what I took away instead were lots of great variables and questions to ask about data that can weed out the information you don’t need and emphasize the more important parts. I’m glad I’m not in the business of data visualization.

Ionic SDK
Jeremy Mason and Josh Durham

Unfortunately for me, I’ve seen these guys do this talk before, but I very much believe in what they preach. They should just work for Ionic so they can go show people how cool it is. Or, I suppose, this session holds more weight in other people’s minds because they don’t. Food for thought…

Intro to Hacking with Raspberry Pi
Sarah Withee

This week, Sarah made hundreds of developers feel the feelings they’ve all been avoiding. We all have a Raspberry Pi at home that we hooked up as a web server, stuck in a corner, and haven’t touched ever again. I have two.

The difference is - Sarah planted little seeds in everyone’s mind with little ideas of what they should go home and try. I wish I had enough spare cash to buy a bunch of fun toys to feed my hobby, but none-the-less.

I will eventually finish this post… soon.

TLDR: While every session left me with something I was not expecting, they were still not what I was expecting. Regardless, I got my money’s worth. Also, I follow a lot more people on Twitter.
