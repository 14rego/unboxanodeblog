One of the reasons that I was hired on at Softek was because we are a technology company - a healthcare software company. But, as of 2010, our website consisted of .jpg images and inline, absolutely-positioned divs and tables in html.

This cannot happen, people. How is it that, in 2012, I can go to a website of a fantastic software company (full of geeks and nerds) and their website looks like a teenager built it with Geocities?

I’m aware that companies are scared of a CMS. Although fantastic for companies that don’t employ nerds to run it, companies are afraid that a templated site will make it looked "canned" and unoriginal, but seriously - instead you chose to hire someone to custom-build you that?! Such a shame. I don’t understand.
